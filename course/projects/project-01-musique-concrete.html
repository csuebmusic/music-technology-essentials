Project 1: Musique concrète–light piece

Presented Wed Sep 16, 2026 (Wk 5)

Build a 2-minute piece entirely from source sounds provided on the NAS. The piece should demonstrate the editing and mixing skills you've learned in Module 2. There is no stylistic requirement beyond "use the source sounds creatively": your piece can be rhythmic, abstract, narrative, atmospheric, or anything else you can imagine.

This is your first piece in the course. The point is not to make a polished commercial track, it's to use the techniques you've learned to make something with intention, where every cut, fade, and mix decision is yours.

Constraints

  • Length: 2 minutes maximum (90 seconds minimum)
  • Source material: Only sounds from the project sample bank at /music/shared/sample-banks/project1/. You may use any sound from the bank, in any combination, processed in any way.
  • Software: Audacity
  • Format: Final export as WAV, 44.1 kHz, 16-bit (we'll cover what those mean)
  • Filename: lastname-project01.wav (in your lastname/project-01/ folder, locally and on the NAS)

What "demonstrating skills" means

A successful piece will show that you:

  • Listened first: chose source sounds with intention rather than randomly
  • Edited deliberately: cuts, fades, and time placements are choices, not accidents
  • Built structure: the piece has shape, it goes somewhere, it isn't 2 minutes of one texture
  • Mixed thoughtfully: levels are balanced, frequencies have room to breathe, stereo placement is considered
  • Used the tools you've learned: the techniques covered in Module 2 are visible in the piece

You don't need to use every technique. A piece that masterfully uses three editing techniques is stronger than one that crams in every effect Audacity offers.

Process expectations

This is a project-based class, and the process matters as much as the product:

  • Start early. First experiments happen in the Wed Wk 3 lab. Don't wait until Wk 5.
  • Save versions. lastname-project01-v1.wav, v2, v3. You'll want to be able to go back.
  • Listen on different headphones if possible. What sounds great on one set of headphones may sound off on another.
  • Take breaks. Listen with fresh ears. Most mixing decisions get worse the longer you stare at the screen without resting.
  • Ask for feedback during lab time. The TA and your classmates are resources.

Presentation (Wed Sep 16)

Each student will play their piece for the class on Wed Wk 5. Presentations are short (just play the piece) but plan to share one sentence about your piece beforehand. Something like: "I built this around the contrast between sharp metallic sounds and long water textures." or "I tried to make a narrative arc, chaotic to calm." Just one sentence to set the listener up.

After each piece, a few seconds of silence, then we move on. No critique in the room: the listening response form (separate) is where written feedback happens.

Rubric

Project 1 is graded out of 100 points across five dimensions, equally weighted at 20 points each.

Source material use (20)

  • Sounds chosen with intention, not at random
  • Variety used effectively (or limitation used effectively, if intentional)
  • Source material is recognizable as such, transformed creatively, or somewhere in between (your call, but defensible)

Editing craft (20)

  • Cuts are clean: no clicks, pops, or accidental cutoffs
  • Fades used where needed: no abrupt unintended starts/stops
  • Selections are precise: silence between events is intentional
  • Audacity techniques from Module 2 are visible in the piece

Mixing craft (20)

  • Levels are balanced: nothing is buried, nothing is overpowering
  • Frequencies have space: EQ used to give elements room
  • Stereo placement is considered
  • Dynamics treatment (if used) supports the piece, doesn't crush it

Structure & form (20)

  • The piece has shape: beginning, middle, end (in some form)
  • Time is used intentionally: pacing decisions are visible
  • 2 minutes feels like the right length for what was built (not artificially padded or rushed)

Process & craft (20)

  • Project folder is organized: versions saved, files named per convention
  • Final export is correctly formatted (WAV, 44.1/16, named correctly)
  • Submitted on time, presented on the assigned day
  • Evidence of revision: the final piece is clearly not a first draft

What I'm not grading

  • Style or genre. A noisy abstract piece and a rhythmic structured piece can both score 100.
  • Technical perfection. Module 2 is the first time most of you have done any of this. Effort and attention matter more than polish.
  • Length within the range. A 95-second piece and a 2-minute piece are both fine. Don't pad to hit a number.
  • Whether I personally like the piece. The rubric is the rubric.

Late policy

Late submissions lose 10 points per day late. Pieces not submitted by the start of class on Wed Sep 16 are considered late. If something serious is going on, talk to the instructor before the deadline. Extensions are possible when communicated in advance.

Drawing inspiration

Listening for the module covers musique concrète. Consider how the composers we listen to use:

  • Source sound choice
  • Repetition vs. variation
  • Silence as a structural element
  • Density vs. sparseness over time
  • Sounds with strong envelopes (sharp attacks, long sustains, etc.), listen with this lens

You're not trying to imitate them. You're trying to learn from how they treat sound as material.

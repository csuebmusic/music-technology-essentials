End-of-Session Checklist

Run through this every time before you leave the lab.

Lab machines are shared. Leaving things half-saved or signed in causes problems for the next person — and for you, when you can't find your work next time. The checklist below takes about two minutes. Do it every session.

1 — Save your work in two places

You've been working from your folder on the NAS. The NAS is your source of truth — that's where your work officially lives. But also keep a local backup, in case the NAS is ever down or you want to use a different computer next time.

  1. Make sure your current work is saved on the NAS. (Cmd + S in whatever app you've been using.)
  2. Open Finder.
  3. Find your lastname/ folder on the NAS, in the Finder sidebar.
  4. Find or create a folder at ~/Documents/lastname/ on the local computer. (In Finder's sidebar, click Documents; if your folder isn't there, make one.)
  5. Drag the day's project folder from the NAS into ~/Documents/lastname/ to copy it. Hold Option while dragging if Finder tries to move instead of copy.
Why two copies

The NAS copy is the official one — it's what the next session will use, and what gets graded. The local copy is your safety net, and it lets you walk to a different lab station next session and still find your work. Don't rely on the local copy alone — different machines have different local folders, and these get wiped between semesters.

2 — Sign out of anything you signed into

This is mostly browser stuff. Whatever you logged into during the session, log out of now.

  • Canvas — click your profile, then Logout.
  • Email or anything else — log out before closing the tab.
  • Google, Microsoft 365, or any account you signed into — sign out, don't just close the window.
  • When done, quit your browser (Cmd + Q) — closing the window isn't enough.
Why this matters

The lab Mac account is shared. The next student to sit at this station logs into the same account you did. If you don't sign out of Canvas (or whatever you used), they'll have access to your account.

3 — Eject the NAS

  1. Find the NAS in Finder's sidebar under Locations.
  2. Click the small eject icon (⏏) next to its name.

This disconnects you from the server cleanly. The next student will reconnect with their own credentials.

4 — Quit your apps

Don't just close windows. Quit the apps so they aren't running in the background.

  • Audacity, Ableton, QuickTime, your browser — each one: Cmd + Q, or use the app's menu in the menu bar.
  • Look at the Dock. If an app has a small dot under its icon, it's still running. Click it and quit.

5 — Leave your station clean

  • Headphones back where you found them, cable not tangled.
  • Mic positioned roughly where it was at the start.
  • Audio interface knobs back to zero (it's the same starting state for the next person).
  • Push the chair in.

The full sequence — quick version

  1. Save your work on the NAS.
  2. Copy your work to ~/Documents/lastname/.
  3. Sign out of Canvas and any other accounts. Quit your browser.
  4. Eject the NAS from Finder's sidebar.
  5. Quit all apps (Cmd + Q).
  6. Knobs to zero. Headphones away. Chair in.

Two minutes. Every session.

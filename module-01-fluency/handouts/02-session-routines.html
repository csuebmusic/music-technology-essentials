Session Routines

How you start and end every session in the lab.

In this class, you work locally on whichever lab computer you're sitting at, and use the NAS to keep your work in sync between sessions. This card has two halves: what to do when you arrive, and what to do before you leave. Both halves take a couple of minutes. Do them every time.

How the workflow works

You always have two copies of your work:

  • Local: at ~/Documents/lastname/ on whichever lab computer you're using. This is where you actually work during a session. Local files load fast and are reliable.
  • NAS: at /music/students/lastname/ on the lab server. This is the master copy. It's how your work moves between computers.

The pattern is: download from NAS at the start, upload to NAS at the end. The local copy is where you work; the NAS is how you transport.

The most important rule

Always upload to the NAS before you leave. If you forget, your work is stranded on whatever computer you used, and the next time you sit at a different computer you'll be working from an older version.

Start of session

Do this when you sit down, before opening any creative software.

  1. Connect to the NAS: in Finder, hold Cmd + K, enter the server address, sign in.
  2. In Finder, navigate to your lastname/ folder on the NAS.
  3. Open another Finder window (Cmd + N) and navigate to ~/Documents/.
  4. Look for a lastname/ folder in ~/Documents/.
    • If it exists: compare with the NAS version. If the NAS version is newer (check modification dates), drag the NAS folder into ~/Documents/ to update your local copy. macOS will ask because the folder already exists; choose Replace (not Keep Both, which creates duplicates).
    • If it doesn't exist (you're at a new computer, or this is your first session): drag your lastname/ folder from the NAS into ~/Documents/ to download it.
  5. Eject the NAS from Finder's sidebar, you don't need it mounted during the session.
Checking modification dates

In Finder, switch to list view by clicking the second icon at the top of the window (or pressing Cmd + 2). This shows the date modified next to each item. If the dates match, your two copies are already in sync: nothing to download. If the NAS is newer, download. If the local is newer, you didn't upload last time. Fix that immediately by skipping ahead to the End-of-session steps and uploading to NAS first.

Now you're ready to work. Open Audacity, Ableton, or whatever you're using, and work entirely from your local ~/Documents/lastname/ folder.

End of session

Do this before you leave the lab. Every session.

  1. Save your work in whatever app you've been using (Cmd + S).
  2. Connect to the NAS again: Cmd + K in Finder.
  3. Navigate to your lastname/ folder on the NAS in one Finder window, and to ~/Documents/lastname/ in another.
  4. Drag your lastname/ folder from ~/Documents/ onto the NAS. macOS will ask: "An item named lastname already exists in this location." Choose Replace: your local version is the one with today's work.
  5. Confirm the upload finished. The folder on the NAS should now show today's modification date.
  6. Eject the NAS from Finder's sidebar.
  7. Sign out of any browser accounts (Canvas, Google, Microsoft, etc.) and quit your browser (Cmd + Q).
  8. Quit all apps with Cmd + Q. Look at the Dock: any app with a small dot under its icon is still running.
  9. Audio interface knobs back to zero. Headphones away. Chair in.
Don't choose "Keep Both"

When you upload and macOS asks whether to Replace, the dialog also offers Keep Both. Don't pick that: it'll create lastname and lastname 2 on the NAS, and the next session you'll have no idea which is current. Always choose Replace when uploading at end of session.

If two copies disagree

If you ever sit down and realize the local copy and NAS copy seem out of sync (different file sizes, different folder contents), pause and figure out which is newer before doing anything.

  • Check modification dates in Finder list view (Cmd + 2).
  • Whichever is newer is the one you trust. Copy it over the older one.
  • Never delete a folder until you're sure you've got the current version somewhere safe.
  • If you can't tell, don't guess. Ask the TA before deleting anything.

Quick versions

Start

Connect to NAS → download lastname/ to ~/Documents/ → eject NAS → work.

End

Save → connect to NAS → upload lastname/ from ~/Documents/ → eject NAS → sign out of browser → quit apps → knobs to zero.
